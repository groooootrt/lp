Frequently asked questions about Avast

What type of company is Avast?
Avast is a publicly traded (LSE: AVST) cybersecurity company offering security, privacy, and productivity software that protects people from threats on the internet and the evolving IoT threat landscape. While most of our primary services are offered under the Avast and AVG brands, the company additionally markets productivity software through its subsidiary, CCleaner.

Where is Avast headquartered?
Avast is headquartered in Prague, Czech Republic.

Who are Avast’s founders?
Pavel Baudis and Eduard Kucera founded Avast, formerly known as Alwil Software, in 1988. Baudis was inspired to develop antivirus software after obtaining a sample of the Vienna Virus at a computer science conference.

Who is Avast’s CEO?
Ondrej Vlcek was appointed Chief Executive Officer at Avast in July 2019. Vlcek joined Avast in 1995 as an intern and held key positions including Chief Technology Officer and Head of Product before ascending to his current position. He is the company’s third CEO.

Do Android phones get viruses?
Android phones don’t get viruses, but your phone can certainly get infected by malware. Android devices have been attacked by adware, ransomware, and various security exploits and zero-day attacks. Protect your Android device against malware, theft, and other threats with Avast Mobile Security for Android.

How can I check to see if my phone has a virus?
Here are a few signs that your phone has a virus or other malware:

  1. Your phone suddenly seems slower
  2. Apps frequently freeze or crash
  3. Your battery is draining faster than usual
  4. Your phone keeps overheating
  5. You’re seeing lots of pop-up ads
  6. There are unfamiliar apps on your phone
  7. You’ve noticed an increase in data usage or getting higher phone bills

Can a hacker shut down your internet?
Yes, a hacker could shut down your internet connection, but they're very unlikely to do so. Hackers who are also cybercriminals are typically focused on stealing money or data, and shutting down your internet makes that impossible for them.

Can someone hack your computer Wi-Fi?
Yes, Wi-Fi connections can be hacked, or interrupted in a number of ways. If you have a private internet network at home, it’s important to keep your router up to date with the latest firmware and to set a strong administrator password. If you frequently use free, public Wi-Fi, use a VPN to ensure that your connection is encrypted and be sure you’ve got an antivirus on your computer or phone.
