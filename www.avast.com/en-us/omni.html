Cyber threats are everywhere

Top threats for PC

From classic viruses and sophisticated phishing attacks to payment-demanding ransomware, reliable PC security has never been more essential.

Ransomware — A dangerous form of malware that locks you out of your PC and demands a ransom in order to regain access.

Phishing attacks — When hackers trick you into handing over sensitive information by pretending to be a legitimate service or organization.

Viruses and worms — Replicating malicious software that spread throughout your computer to steal personal information and wreak havoc.

Top threats for Mac

Keeping your Mac safe means blocking malware and securing your Wi-Fi connection to protect you from online threats.

Malware — Short for malicious software, malware comes in many different forms, including viruses, spyware, Trojans, and many more.

Unsafe websites — Websites that may contain malware, browser tracking, or that may try to send you emails with malicious attachments.

Unsecured Wi-Fi — Connecting to an unsecured public Wi-Fi network can leave you vulnerable to hackers who want to steal your personal data.

Top threats for Android

Hackers often target Android devices via Trojans, ransomware, adware, and more.

Trojan downloader — A type of malware that downloads malicious programs onto your device without you knowing.

Locker ransomware — Locks you out of your device and demands a ransom in order to regain access.

Adware — Floods your device’s screen with unwanted advertisements and pop-ups.

Top threats for iOS

Online threats, such as unsecured Wi-Fi, can leave your security and privacy at risk, resulting in stolen passwords and other personal information.

Unsecured Wi-Fi — Connecting to an unsecured public Wi-Fi network can leave you vulnerable to hackers who want to steal your personal data.

Compromised passwords — A compromised password can enable hackers to access your online accounts and steal your personal data.

Online privacy — Going online without encrypting your connection can let third parties see what you’re doing online.

A few things to know before you order

Unboxing Avast Omni

What is Avast Omni?
Avast Omni is a powerful security solution that includes a software application, as well as the Avast Omni hub, a hardware component that can be easily incorporated into your existing home network via a LAN (ethernet) port on your router. Through the Avast Omni app, you can conveniently monitor all of the devices connected to your home Wi-Fi network and be notified of any detected cyber threats. The app also protects your computers, tablets, and mobiles with our most powerful on-device security, and helps you keep your children safe with comprehensive parental controls.

What features does Avast Omni include?

Avast Omni uses AI and real-time threat detection to protect your home network and all of the devices connected to it against privacy data leakage, malware threats, and device hijacking. The Avast Omni hub connects directly to your router and is managed through the Avast Omni app, which will alert you when a potential threat is detected so that malicious attacks can be shut down before any harm is done.

The Avast Omni app also protects your computers, tablets, and mobiles with our most powerful on-device security software, ensuring that your devices are completely secured against malware and other online threats.

Finally, the Avast Omni app includes parental controls so you can protect your children and help them develop healthy online habits. Block offensive or inappropriate content or apps, set internet time limits, pause the internet temporarily, use geo-location services to check your child’s location and receive alerts when they leave or arrive at specific places (home, school, etc).

How does Avast Omni protect my devices?

Avast Omni gives you three levels of protection for your home and family: home network protection, on-device security, and parental controls. The Avast Omni hub provides protection at the network level (via hardware that connects to your router), while the Avast Omni app provides our most powerful security software for on-device protection on your Windows, Mac, Android, and iOS devices. The Avast Omni app also gives you access to parental controls, so you can protect your children both online and off.

We use artificial intelligence to learn how to detect unusual behavior in your home network and immediately notify you of any malicious activity. By continuously learning the behavior of new devices and processing big data in real-time, Avast Omni provides you with world-class security, privacy, and family protection features.

Does Avast Omni replace my current router?
No. The Avast Omni hub is a hardware component dedicated to protecting your network, that you plug into your router through a provided ethernet cable.

Will Avast Omni slow down my internet?
No. Avast Omni does not affect your router performance and does not slow down your internet speed.

How do the parental control and family safety features work?

In the Avast Omni app, a user profile is created for each person in your household and the devices they use are connected to their profile. Each user profile is tagged as either an administrator, adult, or child. As an administrator, you can use the Avast Omni app to set and control various settings on the other devices connected to your household. The following parental control features are available for child profiles:

  • Geo-location tracking: view the location of your child’s primary device and receive location alerts so you can check that your child is safe at all times
  • Content filters: set parameters for a user profile to block all offensive or inappropriate websites and apps
  • Disable internet: temporarily pause internet access for a user, either on demand or for scheduled times

How big is the Avast Omni hub?
The Avast Omni hub is a small cylindrical disc approximately 0.87 inches high and with a diameter of 3.54 inches.

What do I need to set up Avast Omni?

In addition to the Avast Omni hub that is shipped to you after purchase, you will need:

  • An available LAN (ethernet) port on your network router to connect to the Avast Omni hub. An ethernet cable will be provided with the Avast Omni hub.
  • A nearby electrical outlet to connect the Avast Omni hub to your household electricity (AC power supply). A power cable will be provided with the Avast Omni hub.
  • An Avast Account
  • The Avast Omni app installed on a Windows device. Installation happens automatically after you pre-order Avast Omni.

    • Note: After installation of the Avast Omni app, you will need to restart your computer.

Where is Avast Omni available?
Currently, Avast Omni is only available for US-based customers with a compatible router.

Can I connect Avast Omni wirelessly?
No, you can only connect the Avast Omni hub to your router using an ethernet cable. An ethernet cable is provided with your product when it arrives.

What are the system requirements for Avast Omni?
The Avast Omni app is available for the following operating systems:
PC: Windows 10, 8.1, 8, or 7 (SP2) (32 or 64-bit)
Mac: macOS 10.10 (Yosemite) or higher
Android: Android 5.0 (Lollipop, API 21) or higher
iPhone/iPad: iOS 11.0 or higher

How can I order Avast Omni?
Before ordering Avast Omni, we strongly recommend checking your router compatibility. If your router model is not compatible, we cannot guarantee Avast Omni will work with your home network.

You can check your router compatibility using the Avast Omni trial for Windows or by following the on-screen instructions in your existing Avast Antivirus after clicking on the Avast Omni offer banner. If compatible, continue to follow the on-screen instructions to order Avast Omni.

Note: When you order Avast Omni via your existing Avast Antivirus, or download the Avast Omni trial, any Avast Antivirus you already have installed will be automatically upgraded to Avast Omni.

What happens after I order Avast Omni?
After ordering, you will receive an order confirmation email and in the Avast Omni app you can view the expected shipping date for your Avast Omni hub. While we’re getting your Avast Omni hub sent out to you, you can start setting up user profiles in the Avast Omni app for the people in your household who connect to your Wi-Fi.

When will I get my Avast Omni hub?
Shipping typically takes 5-7 days, but it can take up to 2 weeks for your Avast Omni hub to arrive. You can track the delivery status using the tracking number in the shipping confirmation email we send you.

What if I change my mind?
When you order Avast Omni, you receive an order confirmation email. This email contains instructions for canceling your order if you change your mind before your Avast Omni hub is shipped to you.

If later on, you decide you no longer want Avast Omni, just cancel your subscription. You can put the Avast Omni hub in your local recycling bank for electrical goods.
